How SealMetrics Works

Does SealMetrics prioritize KPIs over legality?​

Not at all. SealMetrics takes privacy a step further.
We don’t track anything that could compromise user privacy.

If tracking a particular KPI would violate users’ rights, we don’t track it — even if regulations technically allow it.

This principle ensures our technology remains privacy-first, not performance-first.

What is SealMetrics’ privacy policy?​

Our stance has been clear from the very beginning:
For us, privacy is not just a policy — it’s a philosophy.

Many of our team members are marketers. When we built SealMetrics, we wanted a web analytics platform that:

  • Respects both user and company privacy
  • Remains effective for data-driven decisions
  • Avoids any identifiable data collection

We intentionally apply stricter privacy standards than some regulations require.
This guarantees that users, professionals, and companies can rely on SealMetrics as a fully compliant, ethical analytics solution.

What regulations does SealMetrics follow?​

Before developing SealMetrics, we consulted with privacy lawyers and data protection specialists to ensure long-term compliance and resilience against future privacy laws.

SealMetrics fully aligns with the principles of:

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
  • ePrivacy Directive
  • CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)
  • PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations)
  • Any upcoming privacy frameworks with similar goals

All these frameworks share a core objective — protecting citizens’ privacy as a fundamental human right.

What SealMetrics Doesn’t Do​

SealMetrics does not track individual journeys or personal activities.
Our system is designed so that no data can ever be linked to a specific person.

That means:

  • No personal identifiers
  • No user profiles
  • No behavioral tracking
  • No data re-identification possible

SealMetrics delivers meaningful, aggregated analytics — without ever compromising privacy.
