# Does SealMetrics comply with CNIL guidelines for analytics without consent?

**Yes. SealMetrics complies with the requirements set by the French CNIL to operate in “consent exemption” mode for audience measurement.**

This means you can legally measure traffic and conversions in France **without showing a cookie banner**, as long as SealMetrics is used exclusively for exempted purposes:

- audience measurement  
- technical performance  
- service optimization  

---

## CNIL Requirements (and How SealMetrics Complies)

### **Limited purpose**
SealMetrics is used strictly for **audience measurement**—not advertising, profiling, retargeting, or monetization.  
👉 

### **No cross-site tracking**
SealMetrics only uses **first-party identifiers** and session IDs.  
No third-party cookies. No shared identifiers across domains.  
👉 

### **IP anonymization**
SealMetrics does **not handle IP addresses** by default.  
👉 

### **Cookie lifetime ≤ 13 months**
Session identifiers are temporary and **do not reset** with revisits.  
👉 

### **Data retention ≤ 24 months**
Older data is automatically deleted or aggregated.  
👉 

### **No mixing with other systems**
Data collected in SealMetrics **is never merged** with CRM databases, profiles, or external datasets.

### **Segregated client data**
Each customer’s dataset is isolated. No cross-client access.

### **User information + opt-out**
Visitors can **opt out** of analytics via browser settings or ad blockers. Since Sealmetrics collects only aggregate statistics without individual identification, standard browser controls satisfy this requirement.
👉 

### **No reuse for SealMetrics’ own purposes**
We never use analytics data to train models, improve algorithms, or for any secondary purpose without consent.  
👉 

### **EU data hosting + controlled transfers**
All servers are located in the **EU**.  
When transfers are required, they are covered by GDPR mechanisms.  
👉

## Important to Know

- CNIL’s exemption applies **only to audience measurement**.  
  Combining analytics with **eCommerce tracking, attribution, or profiling** may require consent.
